About the Pokot Research Centre

The Pokot Resource Centre is a new-to-build health and environment knowledge centre. It reaches out to the people of the isolated Pokot tribe with empowering awareness programs about HIV prevention, female genital mutilation, malaria and personal hygiene. The project also embeds a program on sustainable use of wind, sun and water. It is a combined effort of architecture students of TU Delft, the Kenyan East Pokot Medical Project, local constructors and the Pokot community. The Resource Centre is part of an integrated compound that consists of:
  • A fully equipped medical clinic that provides advanced medical support to 9.000 people;
  • A fitted field ambulance unit with a radius that services up to 10.000 people;
  • A knowledge and awareness centre that facilitates a library, internet, research and presentation means;
  • Accommodation for about 150 overnight attendees;
  • Rain water processing facilities for drinking, irrigation and educational purposes;
  • Wind and solar powered energy generators;
  • Garbage incinerator;
  • Public sanitation.
This small-scale-big-impact project is already underway, with a successful fundraising campaign in Kenya and a pilot crowdfunder in The Netherlands. We’re half-way there.
